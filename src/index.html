About Us

Welcome to the Girls Who Surf Podcast, a series that focuses on the uncovering and elevation of the voices of female surfers in Southern California. Rooted in the research conducted in "Women‚Äôs Experiences in the Surfing Community of Southern California" by Benjamin Morehouse, this podcast emerges as an auditory journey to compliment this work.

The "A Socioeconomic and Recreational Profile of Surfers in the United States" report by Surf-First and the Surfrider Foundation, authored by G. Scott Wagner, Chad Nelsen, and Matt Walker in July 2011, provides an in-depth analysis of the demographics and behaviors of surfers in the United States. One key finding of the report, which focuses on women surfers in Southern California, is the gender composition of the surfing community. The report states:

"The results of the survey indicate that respondents are adults in their early 30s who are educated and earn a high income. We find that surfers are male (90%), 34 years old, educated (62% have a Bachelor‚Äôs degree or above), and employed full-time earning $75,000 per year."

Latest Episodes

Episode 5: Surfing in SoCal vs other internation places

In the fifth episode, I interview Sophia S. Sophia is a surfer from the UK who, before moving to Malibu, spent tremendous amounts of time abroad. She became intertwined with a surfing community in Ecuador after college, and then moved to the US afterwards. In this episode, we talk about her time surfing in London, Ecuador and Malibu and how the communities compare. We then delve into her time longboarding, as well as her experience with localism and men in Malibu.

Episode 4: Huntington Beach Surf Culture and Marine Biology

I interview Sophia B., a local and native of Huntington Beach. Sophia, a student at USC, spends her time surfing, going to the gym, and hopes to be a Marine Biologist. We also talk about surfing's role in childhood development, Huntington Beach Pier Surf culture, the gender gap in surfing and how to feel more comfy as a woman out in the water.

Episode 3: SoCal vs NorCal, women's competition, and other sports

In this episode, I talk with Laurel W., a student at UC Davis, about her experience in Northern California and how it compares to growing up surfing in Southern California. We also touch on female competition surfing and how it differs from men's comp, and how surfing culture changed her mindset on the outdoors while growing up with the sport. We also touch on her experience in high school culture and how she and her team were perceived in being on the surf team.

Episode 2: HB Pier Culture & Sending It as a Woman

In episode two, I talk with a current USC student named Emma, whom is a native of Huntington Beach. Emma talks about her experience with locals in HB, pier culture, and how to get other women to feel comfortable to try surfing for the first time.

Episode 1: San Diego vs Los Angeles Surf

In the first episode of this podcast, I interview Dilan. Dilan is a USC student on the Surf Team, an engineering major, and a San Diego local. We talk about how to get women out on the lineup more, San Diego surf spots, growing up surfing, and surf stereotypes.
